Earnings

How you get paid.

Commission-based, structured fairly, and put in writing before you start. No vague promises.

What the commission covers

Three earning opportunities per school — discussed and agreed at the onboarding call.

01

Setup fee referral

Every new school pays a one-time onboarding fee to get onto EduManage. You earn a cut of that fee for every school you bring in — paid once the school is confirmed and set up.

02

First-year subscription commission

Schools pay an annual subscription based on their student population. You earn a percentage of that first-year subscription for each school that signs through you.

03

Volume bonus

Partners who bring in multiple schools within a single school term unlock an additional bonus on top of the base commission. The more active your network, the more it compounds.

Exact figures are agreed at the onboarding call — before you commit to anything. We put the terms in writing. Commissions are paid within 14 days of a school's subscription being confirmed and payment received.
The process

Four steps from application
to your first commission.

01

Fill in the application form

Takes about 4 minutes. We ask about your school network, which counties you cover, and what draws you to this opportunity. There's one concrete question at the end: name a school you could book a demo at within two weeks. That question tells us more than anything else on the form.

02

We review and shortlist

We review applications within 3 business days and reach out to strong candidates by WhatsApp. We keep this first cohort small — quality over coverage. Everyone gets a response. No ghosting.

03

Partner onboarding call

A 30-minute conversation with the Ansai team. We walk you through EduManage, agree commission terms in writing, and make sure you're fully equipped to represent it. You leave the call ready — with materials, talking points, and a direct line to the product team.

04

Go to market

You start reaching out. We support your first few school conversations directly — demo materials, a school-facing one-pager, and live product support for early calls. You're not selling blind.
